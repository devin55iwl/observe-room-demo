UI Overview:

Purpose: Design a timeline that helps observers monitor and assess participant responses in a research interview in real-time.

Style: Clean, professional, and research-focused.

Key Elements:

Timeline Bar: Display each question as a segment with status icons indicating the signal.

Signal Indicators: Show different icons for different cues, like hesitation, strong reaction, confusion, and engagement drop.

Colors: Use green for high engagement, yellow for medium, and gray for low.

Hover State: When hovering over a question bar, display a one-liner summary about the detected cue (e.g., "⚠ Hesitation detected").

Detailed Requirements:

Timeline Bar:

Display questions as individual segments:

Q1, Q2, Q3, etc.

Bar Color:

🟩 for high engagement (green)

🟨 for moderate engagement (yellow)

⬜ for low engagement (gray)

Icon for Cues:

⚠ for hesitation

★ for strong reaction

❓ for confusion

↓ for engagement drop

When hovering over the question, show a summary of the cue.

Example: "⚠ Hesitation detected: Participant paused for a long time before answering."

Summary Tooltip:

A tooltip will appear when hovering over a question bar, summarizing the cue detected. Keep it concise.

Example: "Q3: How easy was onboarding?"

Engagement: High

Signal: ★ Strong reaction detected

Key moment: Participant reacted strongly when describing the first-time setup.

Question Progress:

Show the progress of each question in the timeline:

Completed questions: Show with an icon representing the cue.

Ongoing questions: Highlight the active segment.

Upcoming questions: Show in gray (inactive).

Section Summary:

Optional: A section summary showing the overall signals for a section (e.g., Onboarding).

Example: Section Summary for Onboarding:

Engagement: High

Confusion: Low

Hesitations: 2

Most reactive moment: “First time setup”
